## Project Overview
Generative AI systems create new opportunities for users, but they also introduce evolving misuse risks that can scale quickly if not identified early. In Trust and Safety environments, analysts must move beyond reactive reporting and help define proactive detection frameworks that support product, engineering, policy, and legal decision-making.

This case study simulates how an analyst might evaluate AI misuse patterns, measure operational risk, identify emerging threat behaviors, and recommend scalable mitigation strategies for a large technology platform.

## Business Objective
The objective of this project is to design an analytics framework that helps a Trust and Safety team:

- detect potentially harmful or policy-violating AI interactions
- identify high-risk user behavior patterns
- monitor abuse trends over time
- support faster and more targeted interventions
- provide cross-functional stakeholders with actionable, defensible insights

## Scenario
A large technology company has launched a generative AI feature. Early signals suggest possible misuse related to spam, harmful content generation, adversarial prompting, and attempts to bypass policy safeguards.

As the analyst supporting Trust and Safety, the goal is to assess the evolving threat landscape and propose a proactive monitoring approach that helps the organization move from reactive enforcement to forward-looking risk mitigation.

## Key Business Questions
This project is designed to answer questions such as:

1. What percentage of AI interactions show signs of abuse or policy violation?
2. Which users or segments contribute disproportionately to risky activity?
3. Are certain prompt patterns or behaviors associated with higher violation rates?
4. How quickly are risky interactions being detected and escalated?
5. What signals could help identify emerging misuse patterns before they scale?

## Dataset
This project uses a generated dataset designed to simulate AI interaction risk signals in a Trust and Safety environment.

Tables include:

- `users`  
  User-level attributes such as account age, region, and prior enforcement history

- `prompts`  
  Prompt-level activity including timestamp, prompt category, and behavioral context

- `model_outputs`  
  Output review outcomes including safety status, violation type, and escalation indicators

- `risk_signals`  
  Derived or collected risk indicators such as anomaly scores, keyword flags, prior user flags, and composite risk factors

## Core Metrics
The analysis is centered around a set of Trust and Safety-oriented KPIs, including:

- **Abuse Rate** — share of AI interactions flagged as policy-violating
- **High-Risk Prompt Rate** — share of prompts containing suspicious intent or elevated risk signals
- **Escalation Rate** — share of interactions requiring human review
- **Repeat Offender Rate** — share of flagged activity generated by previously flagged users
- **Time-to-Detection** — elapsed time between risky interaction and detection
- **Composite Risk Score** — weighted indicator combining behavioral and content-based signals
- **Novel Threat Detection Rate** — share of flagged cases not matching known misuse patterns

## Key Insights

### 1. High Concentration of Unclassified Violations
Approximately 40% of violations fall into a generic “other” category, indicating that the current classification system lacks sufficient granularity to accurately capture emerging misuse patterns. This limits the organization’s ability to track, analyze, and proactively respond to evolving threats.

---

### 2. Disproportionate Impact from Repeat Offenders
Nearly 45% of violations are generated by repeat offenders, suggesting that a relatively small subset of users is responsible for a significant share of harmful activity. This highlights an opportunity to reduce overall platform risk through targeted user-level interventions.

---

### 3. Detection Latency Creates Exposure Risk
The average time-to-detection is approximately 3.37 hours, indicating a meaningful delay between harmful activity and system response. This lag increases the window during which harmful outputs may impact users and suggests that current detection mechanisms may rely too heavily on delayed signals.

---

### 4. Moderate Abuse Rate with Scaled Impact
Approximately 6% of all AI interactions are flagged as violations. While this may appear moderate in percentage terms, at scale it represents a substantial volume of harmful content, reinforcing the need for proactive and targeted mitigation strategies.

## Strategic Recommendations
Based on the final analysis, recommendations will likely focus on areas such as:

- prioritizing high-risk user segments for earlier intervention
- strengthening proactive monitoring with composite risk scoring
- improving escalation workflows for faster review of suspicious activity
- developing better early-warning indicators for emerging misuse patterns
- supporting cross-functional governance for new AI safety risks
